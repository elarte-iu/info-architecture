About Me

Welcome to my webpage! My name is Elizabeth; I'm a second-year MLS student with a (tentative) specialization in Rare Book Librarianship. During my time at IU, I have been especially interested in improving accessibility and "findability" of items of interest to individuals and communities. Paired with my interest in both digital and physical preservation, this has led me to take an approach to my education where I have tried to place an equal amount of focus on learning about the history of books, bookmaking, and librarianship, and how best to describe them, as well as leveraging the technologies of today to make access possible for more people.

Outside of my scholastic aims, my hobbies include creative writing, bird-watching, and knitting (I am in the process of learning how to cable knit and do basic colorwork). I've been challenging myself to become a better cook and baker since starting graduate school, and have made some progress in trying bolder and more complex recipes. Thanks to a passion for geology ignited young, also enjoy collecting rocks, not merely semi-precious stones but also visually interesting samples of sedimentary and igneous rock (and even a couple small fossils!).
